Bringing the Gap Between
People and Technoogy

Communicate and
Collaborate

Clashes and miscommunication between differing perspectives and disciplines can disrupt workflow. The playful nature of the kit brings down walls and naturally incites communication, while rapid iteration and a shared mindset create a flow where everyone can participate on the same level.

Implementing IoT
in Design

The IoT Service Kit presents a comprehensive scope of current technology that enables everyone (tech-oriented or otherwise) to understand and implement IoT.

Focusing iteration on user journeys leads to user-centered design and avoids designing for the cool-factor of technology.

Talking
with Tangibility

The world of IoT is an abstract one, and abstract concepts are difficult to talk about. Simply rearranging tokens on the IoT Service Kit blueprint enables teams to make concepts tangible by physically demonstrating those ideas.

Components of the kit

Blueprints

Blueprints:

The Blueprints provide a general enviromental context.
Create your own to fit your project's needs.

Tokens

3D printed tokens:

The 3D printed tokens are physical representations of the user, touchpoints, interactions, and services. They help visualise ideas.

Cards

Game cards:

The game cards are divided into five categories: Sensors, Interaction, Service Cards, City Open API, and User Cards. They present a scope of technology.

Whatever you
need!

Customise it:

Create whatever you might need and contribute to the scope of the kit!

Hit te Ground Running!

All you need to do is identify the context and environment (choose a board, identify the problem), establish user personas, and start. Alter the environment as you explain and discuss ideas. Document every step of the way!
